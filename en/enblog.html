Dry vs. Wet AMD: Key Differences

November 3, 2025 ~2 min Basics

Dry AMD is more common and typically progresses slowly with “drusen” deposits under the retina. Wet AMD involves abnormal blood vessels that leak; vision can be affected quickly and often needs urgent treatment.

Quick FAQ

My Amsler grid looks abnormal—what should I do?

Don’t panic. This is a screening tool. Arrange an appointment with an eye specialist as soon as possible.

Should I save my results?

Keeping dated notes or screenshots helps you notice changes over time.

Should I do the test with glasses?

Use the same correction you normally wear (glasses/contacts) during the test.
